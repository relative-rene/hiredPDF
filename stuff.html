MID-SIZED COMPANIES OFFER HIGHEST SALARIES: Our data shows that salary and number of employees are positively correlated until we reach the 201-500 employee mark, and then the numbers start to go back down. One possible reason for this is the availability of salary alternatives as part of an overall compensation package. For example, companies with less than 200 people are usually in the series b or below stage, and can offer more equity, whereas many companies with more than 500 people are publicly traded and can offer stock packages. Those in the middle have less equity to give and typically aren’t publicly traded, so higher salaries may be a way to keep offers competitive.
